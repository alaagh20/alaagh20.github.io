Ladies of Harley

Some of us are women who ride our own bikes and some prefer to be a passenger. Ladies of Harley (L.O.H.) is a program sponsored by the Harley Owners Group to support women motorcycle enthusiasts (both riders and passengers). It is not a separate organization within H.O.G., but was established to encourage women to become more active in H.O.G. and their local chapters.

Remember you don't have to have your own bike to be a L.O.H. member. We encourage ladies to participate in chapter events as well as functions specifically for the ladies. We are a very friendly social and supportive group, for the ladies as well as the chapter. We have a lot of fun together and enjoy each other’s company very much. We have organized and participated in functions like L.O.H. dinner meetings, chapter rides and overnight events.

We strive to support at least one charity project per year. This year, as in 2010, we are holding a charity day. This has proved to be, not only a good fundraiser, but also a great deal of fun.

So if you're thinking about joining the Kingdom of Jordan Ladies of Harley, give us a try. You won't know what it's all about and what you are missing if you don't. I invite you to drop by one of our functions whenever you'd like.

How do you become a member of Ladies of Harley? You must first join the International Harley Owners Group by logging to www.hog.com.

You can be either an associate or full member. Once you are a member of H.O.G. you can join our local Chapter. Ladies of Harley membership allows you to participate in all the L.O.H. activities at various H.O.G. rallies and events. These may include workshops, seminars, photo sessions, and other special events. You will receive a Ladies of Harley pin and a patch along with all the Harley Owners Group items. You will be identified as a registered L.O.H. member by having the letters L.O.H. on your H.O.G. card. L.O.H. membership is free to active H.O.G. members.

Remember Ladies of Harley is for ALL female motorcycle enthusiasts, whether you ride on the front or the back of the bike.

We look forward to getting to know you at one of our upcoming events!

Martine Abry, Ladies of Harley Officer
